Picture 5: Would you pick up hitchhikers like these??		 From my father-in-law (who in himself, is a fountain of knowledge!) I learnt many unknown facts about Chile. It is the longest country in the world (4,300Km!) and because of this it has a range of climates - it has hot,arid dry deserts in the North, Mediterranean Climate in the middle and snow in the South. The Andes Mountains6 run along the Eastern Border of the country. Chile also owns Easter Island - a small outpost off the coast of Chile where the Maui figures stand (big heads carved out of stone), although the origins of which still remain a mystery to this very day.
We have already begun on our itinerary for our next visit - and Easter Island is our first stop! Next time we go we'd prefer to go when it's summertime too - on our last trip we did visit Santiago's closest beach (Valparaiso), but it was too cold to go for a swim!

The Millennium (January 1,2000)
Y2K - what a non-event eh? Well not so for myself and others in my industry - unless you were asleep, comatose or dead you would've heard about the 'Millennium Bug'. Primarily associated with older computers not being able to cope with the turning of the clock at midnight, January 1 2000 it kept IT individuals busy in preparation. Most of it involved replacing old hardware. Although 99% of the population saw the Y2K/Millenium Bug as a non-starter, I view it differently ; because of the efforts of the IT personnel around the globe, problems were minimised and/or eliminated. Would people have preferred it if computer systems stopped working then??? :)
